2. What are cookies and similar technologies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit websites. They can be "session" (deleted when you close your browser) or "persistent" (remain for a set period). Similar technologies include local storage, web beacons, and pixel tags. Cookies help the site remember your choices and provide useful features (e.g., remembering your language preference).

3. Why Musika Wedu uses cookies

A. Essential / Strictly necessary cookies

Purpose: Enable core site functionality (account login session, security, OTP flows, crash recovery).

Why no consent required: Necessary to deliver the service you explicitly requested (account, listing creation).

Example: Session identifiers, CSRF tokens, OTP verification session markers.

B. Functional / Preference cookies

Purpose: Remember choices you make (language, display preferences) so the site behaves as you expect.

Consent: Recommended but can be treated as legitimate interest where strictly limited.

C. Performance / Analytics cookies

Purpose: Collect aggregated, anonymised usage statistics to help us improve the site (pages visited, search terms, error rates).

Examples: Google Analytics (or equivalent) session/usage cookies.

Consent: We will ask for your consent for analytics cookies and record that consent.

D. Marketing / Advertising cookies

Purpose: (Only with your explicit consent) deliver relevant marketing, measure ad campaign performance and provide limited remarketing.

Examples: Third-party ad networks or remarketing pixels if/when we run adverts.

Consent: Explicit opt-in required.

E. Third-party cookies / embedded content

Purpose: When we embed maps, videos, or "click-to-chat" links (e.g., WhatsApp, map providers, CDN services), these third parties may set cookies under their own policies. We do not control their cookies — see Section 7.

Examples: Google Maps, CDN analytics, WhatsApp click-to-chat metadata (WhatsApp is a third-party app).

4. Legal basis & Zimbabwean context

  • Zimbabwe's Cyber & Data Protection Act and SI 155/2024 require lawful processing, appropriate safeguards, registration (where required) and appointing a Data Protection Officer for controllers meeting the threshold. SI 155 also sets out breach-notification duties.
  • There is currently no standalone "cookie law" in Zimbabwe similar to the EU ePrivacy Directive, but data protection rules apply to personal data derived from cookies. Legal commentators therefore recommend obtaining consent for non-essential cookies as best practice. (That is the approach Musika Wedu takes.)
  • POTRAZ and local practitioners have emphasised transparency, consent records, and readiness to notify in case of a breach. We will follow that approach.

5. How we obtain, record and keep cookie consent

On first visit we will show a cookie banner that (a) explains cookie categories, (b) allows users to accept all, reject non-essential, or manage preferences, and (c) links to this full Cookie Policy and our Privacy Policy.

If you accept, we store a consent record (which cookie categories you accepted, date/time, your IP and device fingerprint) for audit and compliance. We recommend retaining consent logs for at least 5 years (or as required by SI 155 / POTRAZ guidance).

You can change or withdraw consent at any time using the cookie preference link in the site footer or by clearing cookies in your browser. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing prior to withdrawal.

Recommended cookie banner copy (example):

"We use cookies to make Musika Wedu work, improve the site and show optional personalized content. Essential cookies are required. Choose 'Accept All' to allow analytics and marketing cookies, or 'Manage Preferences' to select which cookies you allow. Read our Cookie Policy."

We will record which option you chose and the timestamp.

6. How to manage, block or delete cookies (user controls)

  • Cookie banner: Use "Manage Preferences" to change choices at any time.
  • Browser controls: You can block or delete cookies via your browser settings (Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Edge). Blocking some cookies may break site features (login, listing creation).
  • Mobile devices: Browser settings or device app permissions can be used to clear cookies and site data.
  • Third-party opt-outs: Many analytics and ad networks provide opt-out links or browser plugins (e.g., Google Analytics opt-out add-on). For WhatsApp/phone communications: those are handled in your messaging app — check WhatsApp's privacy settings.

7. Third-party cookies & embedded content

Musika Wedu uses selected third-party providers (edge/CDN, analytics, maps). When you interact with embedded third-party content (maps, videos, WhatsApp click-to-chat), those providers may set cookies. Their practices are governed by their own privacy/cookie policies and not by Musika Wedu. Examples include: CDN providers for images, mapping service for location features, analytics tools, and payment providers (if/when enabled). You should review those third-party policies for details.

8. Retention of cookie data (recommended retention windows)

  • Essential cookies: Session only (deleted after browser close) or up to 30 days where necessary for login/session continuity.
  • Functional cookies: Up to 12 months (to remember preferences).
  • Analytics cookies: Aggregated anonymised data retained for 12–36 months (we will anonymise where possible).
  • Marketing cookies: Up to 24 months unless you withdraw consent sooner.
  • Consent records: Retain for at least 5 years to satisfy audit and SI 155 compliance and to demonstrate lawful basis.

These are recommended retention windows; Musika Wedu will publish exact retention times in the cookie banner and Privacy Policy.

9. Cross-border transfers

If cookie/analytics data is transferred outside Zimbabwe (for example to a cloud analytics provider), Musika Wedu will only transfer where permitted by the Act and SI 155 and will implement safeguards (contractual protections, encryption). POTRAZ guidance and SI 155 require careful handling and notification when transferring personal data abroad — we will follow those rules.

10. Data security & breach handling (cookie-related incidents)

  • Cookie data (and logs) are stored securely with access controls and encrypted where appropriate.
  • SI 155/2024 and guidance require data controllers to notify POTRAZ promptly in the event of a reportable breach. Musika Wedu will follow the statutory breach notification timelines (POTRAZ/SI guidance requires notifying POTRAZ within 24 hours of awareness for reportable incidents and notifying affected individuals where a high risk to rights/freedoms exists, typically within 72 hours).
  • We will also follow our internal incident response plan.

11. Children & cookies

Musika Wedu is intended for adult users (18+). We do not knowingly set cookies based on data collected from children. If you believe a child under 18 provided personal data, contact info@nessosystems.co.zw and we will take appropriate action.

12. Constitutional rights & transparency

We respect Zimbabwe's constitutional protections which underpin data privacy and access to information. For transparency we quote the relevant sections:

Section 57 — Right to privacy

"Every person has the right to privacy, which includes the right not to have— (1) their home, premises or property entered without their permission; (2) their person, home, premises or property searched; (3) their possessions seized; (4) the privacy of their communications infringed; or (5) their health condition disclosed."

Section 62 — Access to information (extract)

"Every person … has the right of access to any information held by any person, including the State, in so far as the information is required for the exercise or protection of a right. … Every person has a right to the correction of information, or the deletion of untrue, erroneous or misleading information …"

Section 64 — Freedom of profession, trade or occupation

"Every person has the right to choose and carry on any profession, trade or occupation, but the practice of a profession, trade or occupation may be regulated by law."

How this affects cookies: Cookies and tracking must be used in ways that respect the privacy of communications and personal data. Where processing affects privacy, we rely on consent or legitimate interest and provide mechanisms to access, correct or delete data (see the Privacy Policy and Data Subject Rights procedures).

13. Practical implementation checklist for the site

  1. Implement a prominent cookie banner on first visit with buttons: Accept all, Reject non-essential, Manage preferences. Record the user's choice plus timestamp/IP/device.
  2. Block analytics/marketing cookies until consent is given (client-side gating). Allow essential cookies before consent where strictly required for login or security.
  3. Provide a persistent cookie preferences link in the footer to update choices.
  4. Log consent records for at least 5 years. Provide an audit trail (who, when, what categories accepted).
  5. Publish a detailed cookie table (category, purpose, provider, retention, type) and link it from the banner.
  6. Implement a DPO contact and POTRAZ notification pathway in your incident response plan (24-hour reporting window).
